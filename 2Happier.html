#2: Marshmello & Bastille - Happier

Description About the Song:

"Happier" by Marshmello featuring Bastille is a poignant song that explores themes of love, sacrifice, and selflessness. The lyrics tell the story of a relationship that is coming to an end, with the narrator expressing a desire for their partner's happiness, even if it means letting them go. The song reflects the emotional struggle of acknowledging the pain of a failing relationship while recognizing that the best way to make someone happy is to let them go. It has been described as a breakup song that balances upbeat music with melancholic lyrics, resonating with listeners who have experienced similar feelings of longing and sacrifice in their own lives.

Description About the Authors:

Marshmello is an American DJ and music producer, known for his distinctive white marshmallow-shaped helmet that conceals his identity. His real name is Christopher Comstock, born on May 19, 1992. Marshmello gained popularity in the electronic dance music (EDM) scene starting in 2015, with a musical style that includes bass-heavy electro-dance music and deep house influences. He is inspired by other masked DJs like Deadmau5 and Daft Punk, and has become one of the most recognizable figures in the industry.

Bastille is an English indie pop band known for their unique fusion of indie pop and rock. Formed in London in 2010, the band initially gained traction through their homemade mixtapes and engaging live performances. They quickly caught the attention of music lovers with their evocative lyrics and rich soundscapes, which set them apart from their peers. The band's lineup consists of four talented members: lead vocalist Dan Smith, keyboardist Kyle Simmons, guitarist Will Farquharson, and drummer Chris Wood. Each member brings distinct contributions, enriching their music’s overall texture. Dan Smith, in particular, has often been credited as the driving force behind the band’s poetic lyricism and stylistic choices. Together, they create a sound that is both anthemic and introspective, appealing to a wide audience across different musical tastes. Bastille’s breakthrough came with the release of their debut album, “Bad Blood,” in 2013, which garnered critical acclaim and commercial success. The album featured the hit single “Pompeii,” which has become synonymous with the band’s name, exemplifying their signature style that meshes emotive melodies with thought-provoking themes. Over the years, Bastille has continued to evolve, experimenting with diverse sounds and genres, as seen in their subsequent albums such as “Wild World” and “Doom Days.” This creative evolution not only showcases Bastille’s artistic growth but also their commitment to reflecting contemporary issues through music.
